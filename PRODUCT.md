# Product

## Register

brand

## Brand Name

Amara Muebles. Slogan: "El arte en tu hogar."

## Users

Two equal channels:

**Consumer (B2C)**: Local Bolivian families, middle-class to high-income homeowners, primary age 40+. Not tech-savvy. Discover via word-of-mouth, Instagram, local search. Want to feel quality before committing. Primary action: send a WhatsApp message about a specific piece, or visit the showroom.

**Business (B2B)**: Interior designers, contractors, boutique hotels, commercial decorators. Deadline-driven. Need to know quality, pricing range, and how to place project orders quickly. Primary action: send a WhatsApp inquiry or visit the B2B section for bulk/project purchases.

## Product Purpose

Sell artisanal wood furniture (oak/roble, walnut/nogal, teak/teca) made by master craftsmen in Bolivia. Zero backend — no cart, no payment, no accounts. All conversions flow through WhatsApp: each product has a pre-filled WhatsApp message ("Quiero comprar [product name]"), and the contact form sends the visitor's typed message directly to WhatsApp. Success = a WhatsApp conversation started, a showroom visit booked, or a B2B inquiry sent.

## Conversion Model

- **No cart, no checkout, no backend.** Every CTA opens WhatsApp.
- Product buttons: `wa.me/[number]?text=Quiero%20comprar%20[product]` — unique per product.
- Contact form: message textarea only (no name, no email). On submit, opens WhatsApp with the entered message. Validation: message must not be empty.
- Primary funnel: See product → click WhatsApp button → conversation starts.
- Secondary funnel: Browse → feel the brand → open contact form → send message via WhatsApp.

## Brand Personality

Passionate, Premium, Quality-Price relationship. Warm and personal. The brand cares about craft and wants customers to feel the value — honest premium, not cold luxury. Bolivian provenance is a selling point.

## Anti-references

- IKEA: mass-market, flat, transactional catalog feel
- Generic furniture e-commerce (identical card grids, discount badges, aggressive pricing banners)
- Cold corporate design — too polished, too distant, no soul
- Anything that looks like a warehouse or wholesale shop

## Design Principles

1. **Craftsmanship over catalog** — show the soul of the work. Every section should feel like a curated showroom, not a store.
2. **WhatsApp is the counter** — every CTA leads to a conversation. Design should make starting that conversation feel natural and easy.
3. **Clarity for every generation** — 40+ users are primary. Generous type, high contrast, clear hierarchy, no cognitive overload.
4. **Accessible premium** — quality-price relationship is core. Signal high quality without signaling "out of reach."
5. **Both audiences, one voice** — B2C and B2B users share the same brand experience; the B2B section gives contractors their own path without making the consumer experience feel commercial.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Comfortable for users 40+: larger base font, generous line spacing, strong contrast (WCAG AA minimum, AA+ preferred)
- Clear, readable CTAs — no icon-only buttons
- Respect reduced motion preferences
- Generous touch targets (min 44px) — tablet/mobile primary for Carmen
